WaveMaker follows a predictable, transparent, and standard release strategy that balances rapid innovation with enterprise-grade stability.  
Our release model enables teams to confidently adopt new capabilities while ensuring long-term reliability for production applications.

This page provides a high-level overview of how WaveMaker releases work, how versions are managed, and how release policies differ between **WaveMaker Online (WMO)** and **WaveMaker Enterprise (WME)**.

---

## Versioning Philosophy

WaveMaker follows a versioning approach that is closely aligned with **[Semantic Versioning](https://semver.org/)**.
Each WaveMaker release follows the format:

### Version Components

- **MAJOR**  
  Introduced for significant platform-level changes, architectural upgrades, or foundational enhancements.

- **MINOR**  
  Introduced for new features, functional enhancements, and meaningful improvements that remain backward compatible.

- **PATCH**  
  Introduced for bug fixes, performance optimizations, and security updates with no breaking changes.

This structure helps teams clearly understand the **scope, impact, and upgrade expectations** of every release.

---

## Release Cadence

WaveMaker follows a steady and predictable release cadence, with flexibility based on quality and platform readiness.

### Patch Releases
- **Frequency:** Approximately every 2 weeks  
- **Focus:**  
  - Bug fixes  
  - Security patches  
  - Minor enhancements  

### Major & Minor Releases
- **Frequency:** Approximately every 3 months  
- **Focus:**  
  - New features  
  - Platform enhancements  
  - Developer experience improvements  

Wherever possible, **backward compatibility is preserved**, and platform-managed migrations ensure smooth upgrades.

---

## Deployment Models and Release Behavior

WaveMaker release behavior differs based on the deployment model.

### WaveMaker Online (WMO)

WaveMaker Online always runs on the latest available version.

- Updates are **automatically applied**
- No manual upgrades required
- Immediate access to new features and improvements
- Best suited for teams that prefer:
  - Zero maintenance
  - Continuous innovation
  - Always-current platform capabilities

---

### WaveMaker Enterprise (WME)

WaveMaker Enterprise provides **controlled and predictable upgrade cycles**.

- Customers choose **when to upgrade**
- Clearly defined **support lifecycle**
- Includes:
  - Active Support
  - End-of-Life (EOL) notifications
  - Optional Long-Term Support (LTS) releases
- Ideal for:
  - Enterprise governance
  - Regulated environments
  - Structured change management processes

> **Note:**  
> Support lifecycle policies, EOL notifications, and LTS releases apply **only to WaveMaker Enterprise (WME)**.

---

## Support Lifecycle (WME)

WaveMaker Enterprise supports a rolling window of **recent major and minor releases** to ensure security and platform stability.

- Typically supports the **latest three major/minor releases**
- Versions beyond this window are marked **End of Life (EOL)**
- Advance notifications are provided to help plan upgrades
- Selected releases may be designated as **Long-Term Support (LTS)**, focusing on critical fixes and security updates
